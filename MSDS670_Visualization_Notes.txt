The more I read about Edward Tufte, the more I see him associated with the "sparkline" (CITATION), the small trendline that can help to annotate a thought via data, or to show a small relationship between two variables within a passage of text. Edward Tufte seems to be one of the main godfathers of data visualization and data analytics and I'm very interested in reading his book (CITATION) after researching him a bit.

Certainly we have been exposed to concepts which have come directly or indirectly from Tufte already. One aspect of his coverage online was this aspect of "telling the truth with data", or always trying to be the most objective when presenting data. Any bias or stretch of the truth can lead to one's credibility suffering for years and years.

I agreed with the principle of always trying to tell the absolute truth with the data. The concept of high data density and maximizing this "data-ink ratio" also resonated with me in that removing clutter should be one of the main principles of a data scientist. Only displaying the data and helping to draw complex conclusions from the data is one of the main objectives in presenting data to a population.
